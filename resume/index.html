My Awesome Resume

Skills

I can work in any programming language. This is not a joke. My personal philosophy is that a programming language is just a tool. And like any good worker, one should know many tools and have the capacity to quickly learn new ones. After all, you wouldn't hire someone that only knows how to use a hammer when what the job requires is a screwdriver. Like the good worker, I make sure I know many programming languages and have the ability to quickly learn new ones. The same goes for any software technology: databases, libraries, tools... I make it a personal mission to know about as much of these as possible, and I love learning about new ones.

Experience

I've worked for CDM Technologies, Sony Online Entertainment, ngmoco:), Quark Games, KIXEYE, and more. I've done consultation work for Google, Facebook, Amazon, and others. I like contract work most because I'm constantly learning new tech and skills to keep up with the jobs. I've worked front-end, back-end, database, customer support, and worn a few other hats here and there.

Education

I was given a test called the HSEE, or High School Exit Exam, after only one year of high school. The test was described as containing the minimum knowledge required to be considered for graduating high school. I aced it, but they didn't let me exit high school after doing so, which seemed ironic to me. I did the college thing too, but I find that work experience is a better education than any school or college.

Why You Should Hire Me

I'm an extremely fair and hard-working individual. If you have work you need done, or if you just want a consultation about what it is that needs to be done, I will always aim to deliver the best and second-best options. Why do I include second-best? Because I know time, money, and other factors sometimes prevent the absolute best scenario. It's unfortunate, but it's life, and that's why I prepare second-best alternatives.

Aside from that, I also focus on process improvement where possible. To me, programming is the easy part of the development process. It's managing design, task creation, integration, testing, and all that other junk that are the problem areas. So I usually try to enlighten my coworkers and colleagues when appropriate. I also love learning, as I mentioned under the Skills section, so I'm very receptive to when coworkers and colleagues want to enlighten me as well.
